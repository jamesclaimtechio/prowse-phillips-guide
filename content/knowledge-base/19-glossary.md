# Glossary

A comprehensive A-Z reference of terms used in the Prowse Phillips CRM system.

---

## A

### Active (Client Status)
A client who has completed onboarding and made their first payment. Their cases are being actively worked.

### Affordability Assessment
An evaluation of a client's income, expenses, and disposable income to determine what payment plan they can afford.

### APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
The yearly interest rate charged on credit. A key term that must be included in credit agreements.

### Approval
Authorization from a solicitor required before sending certain documents (LOC, settlement proposals, route determinations).

---

## B

### Breach
A failure by a creditor to comply with regulations. Breaches identified in Maxi Reports can support Route U determinations.

---

## C

### Case
An investigation into ONE specific debt. One client may have multiple cases (one per debt).

### Case Handler
See **Handler**.

### Case Status
The current stage of a case in the investigation workflow (e.g., DSAR Sent, Route Determined - U).

### CCA (Consumer Credit Act)
The Consumer Credit Act 1974 - legislation governing credit agreements in the UK. Source of Section 77/78 rights.

### Chase
A follow-up communication sent when a creditor hasn't responded within the expected timeframe.

### Client
A person who has completed onboarding and made their first payment. See also **Lead**.

### Client Status
The client's position in their payment journey with us (e.g., ACTIVE, MISSED_PAYMENT, FULLY_PAID).

### Creditor
A company that is owed money by the client (banks, credit card companies, loan providers).

---

## D

### Default Notice
A formal notice creditors must send under CCA Section 87 before taking enforcement action. Must give 14 days to remedy.

### Direct Debit
An authorization allowing us to collect payments directly from a client's bank account via GoCardless.

### DMP (Debt Management Plan)
A structured plan to repay debts over time, usually managed by a third-party provider. We may refer clients to DMP providers for Route E cases.

### DSAR (Data Subject Access Request)
A formal request under GDPR requiring an organization to provide all personal data they hold about an individual. Creditors must respond within 30 days.

---

## E

### Enforceable
A debt that is legally valid and can be pursued through the courts. See **Route E**.

---

## F

### FCA (Financial Conduct Authority)
The UK regulator for financial services, including consumer credit.

### Fully Paid (Client Status)
A client who has completed all payments under their payment plan.

---

## G

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
EU/UK regulation governing the processing of personal data. Gives individuals rights including data access (DSAR).

### GoCardless
The third-party payment provider we use to process Direct Debit payments.

---

## H

### Handler
The staff member assigned to and responsible for a case. Different handlers may work different stages (pre-lit vs post-lit).

---

## I

### ICO (Information Commissioner's Office)
The UK regulator for data protection. Creditors who fail to respond to DSARs can be reported to the ICO.

### Introducer
An external referral partner who sends us clients in exchange for commission.

---

## L

### Lead
A potential client who has started onboarding but hasn't completed payment setup. See also **Client**.

### Letter of Authority (LOA)
A signed document authorizing us to act on behalf of the client and communicate with their creditors.

### LOC (Letter of Claim)
A formal legal letter asserting that a debt is unenforceable. Sent in Route U cases after solicitor approval.

---

## M

### Magic Link
A passwordless login method used for the client portal. Clients receive a one-time link via email to access their account.

### Mandate
A Direct Debit authorization set up with GoCardless allowing us to collect payments.

### Maxi Report
A third-party analysis report that identifies potential legal breaches in credit agreements. Used to support route determination.

### Missed Payment (Client Status)
A client who has a payment overdue.

---

## N

### Notes
Internal comments added to client or case records by staff. Not visible to clients.

---

## O

### Onboarding
The process of signing up a new client, including collecting details, debts, affordability, and payment setup.

---

## P

### Payment Plan
The agreed schedule for a client to pay our fees. Can be 12-in-12 (exempt), Regulated Credit, or Pay in Full.

### Post-Lit (Post-Litigation)
The stage of case handling after route determination (Stages 3-4). Involves LOC or settlement work.

### Pre-Lit (Pre-Litigation)
The stage of case handling during investigation (Stages 1-2). Involves DSAR, S78, and analysis.

### Prescribed Terms
Specific terms that must be included in a credit agreement under CCA (amount, APR, repayment terms). Missing terms can make the agreement unenforceable.

---

## Q

### Queue
A filtered list of cases or tasks organized by status or type. Used for batch processing similar work.

---

## R

### Regulated Credit
A payment plan that doesn't meet 12-in-12 criteria and is therefore subject to FCA regulation.

### Route (Route U / Route E)
The determination of whether a debt is unenforceable (U) or enforceable (E). Made after investigation is complete.

### Route E
"Enforceable" - The debt is legally valid, but we can help through settlement negotiation.

### Route U
"Unenforceable" - We've found issues that make the debt legally unenforceable. We pursue write-off via Letter of Claim.

---

## S

### Section 77
A provision of the Consumer Credit Act allowing individuals to request account statements for running-account credit.

### Section 78
A provision of the Consumer Credit Act requiring creditors to provide a copy of the executed credit agreement within 12 working days.

### Section 87
A provision of the Consumer Credit Act requiring proper default notices before enforcement action.

### SECCI (Standard European Consumer Credit Information)
A standardized disclosure document required for regulated credit agreements.

### Settlement
An agreement with a creditor to pay less than the full debt amount. Used in Route E cases.

### SRA (Solicitors Regulation Authority)
The regulatory body for solicitors in England and Wales.

### Status
See **Client Status** or **Case Status**.

---

## T

### Task
A specific action that needs to be completed, created automatically by the system or manually by staff.

### Template
A pre-formatted email document with merge variables for consistent creditor communication.

### Timeline
A visual chronological history of events on a client or case record.

### 12-in-12
A payment plan with 12 or fewer instalments, over 12 months or less, with no interest. Exempt from FCA regulation.

---

## U

### Unenforceable
A debt that cannot be legally enforced, typically due to issues with the credit agreement or default process. See **Route U**.

---

## V

### Variable (Template Variable)
A placeholder in email templates (e.g., {{CLIENT_NAME}}) that is replaced with actual values when the email is composed.

### Vulnerable Client
A client who may need additional support due to health, circumstances, or capability. Requires extra care in communication.

---

## W

### Write-Off
When a creditor agrees to cancel a debt. The ideal outcome of a Route U case.

- Awaiting Payment
- Ready to Assess

### DSAR Phase
- DSAR Requested
- DSAR Sent
- DSAR Chase 1/2/3 Sent
- DSAR Response Received
- DSAR Reviewed
- DSAR Complete

- Section 78 Requested
- Section 78 Sent
- Section 78 Chase Sent
- Section 78 Response Received
- Section 78 Reviewed
- Section 78 Complete

### Analysis Phase
- Ready for Maxi Report
- Maxi Report Requested
- Maxi Report Received
- Maxi Report Under Review
- Route Determination Needed

- Route Determined - U
- LOC Drafting
- LOC Pending Approval
- LOC Approved
- LOC Sent
- LOC Chase 1/2 Sent
- LOC Under Negotiation
- Route U - Completed

- Settlement Drafting

- Settlement Sent
- Settlement Under Negotiation
- Route E - Completed

- Case Closed - Successful
- Case Closed - Unsuccessful
- Case Cancelled
