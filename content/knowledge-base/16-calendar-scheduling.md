# Calendar and Scheduling

This guide covers the calendar system - how to manage appointments, schedule callbacks, block time, and set your availability.

---

## Overview

The calendar system helps you:
- Schedule appointments and callbacks with clients
- Block time for non-client work
- Manage your availability
- View your colleagues' calendars
- Track today's calls

## Calendar Views

### Grid View

The default view showing appointments in a grid format:
- **Day view:** Hour-by-hour breakdown
- **Week view:** 7-day overview
- **Month view:** Monthly overview

Switch between views using the buttons at the top of the calendar.

### Navigation

- **Today button:** Jump to current date
- **Arrow buttons:** Move forward/back (day, week, or month)
- **Date picker:** Jump to specific date

### Staff Filter

Use the staff selector to view:
- **My calendar:** Just your appointments
- **Team calendar:** All staff appointments
- **Specific person:** One colleague's calendar

## Types of Calendar Events

### Appointments

Scheduled meetings with clients:

## Managing Callbacks

### What is a Callback?

A callback is a scheduled call, typically:
- Requested by the client through the portal
- Scheduled by staff during a conversation
- Follow-up to a missed call

### Viewing Callbacks

Callbacks appear in:
- Your calendar (as scheduled events)
- **Queues** â†’ **Callbacks** (dedicated queue)
- Today's calls list

### Booking a Callback

When a client requests a callback:

1. Go to **Queues** â†’ **Callbacks** or
2. Create from calendar or client profile
3. Select available time slot
4. Client receives confirmation

### Completing a Callback

After making the call:
1. Open the callback from your calendar or queue
2. Mark as **Completed**
3. Add notes about the outcome
4. The callback is removed from your queue

### Missed Callbacks

If you miss a scheduled callback:
1. It appears in **Queues** â†’ **Missed Calls**
2. You should attempt to reach the client
3. Reschedule or mark as attempted

## Rescheduling and Cancelling

### Rescheduling an Appointment

1. Click on the appointment
2. Click **Reschedule**
3. Select new date/time
4. Confirm

The client receives notification of the change (if applicable).

### Cancelling an Appointment

1. Click on the appointment
2. Click **Cancel**
3. Select reason (optional)
4. Confirm

### Bulk Rescheduling

If you need to reschedule multiple appointments (e.g., unexpected absence):
1. Contact your team lead
2. They can help reassign or reschedule in bulk

## Best Practices

### Keeping Your Calendar Current

1. **Update availability** when it changes
2. **Block time** for non-client work
3. **Complete appointments** after calls
4. **Reschedule promptly** if plans change

### Preparing for Calls

Before an appointment:
1. Review the client/case
2. Check notes from previous contact
3. Know what you need to discuss
4. Have relevant documents ready

### After Calls

1. Mark appointment as complete
2. Add notes about what was discussed
3. Create follow-up tasks if needed
4. Update case status if applicable

### Avoiding Double-Booking

- Check your calendar before scheduling
- Use availability settings correctly
- Block time for meetings/other commitments
- Review tomorrow's calendar end of each day

## FAQ

### How do I see my calls for next week?

Use the calendar navigation to move to next week, or switch to week view.

### Can clients book their own appointments?

Yes, clients can request callbacks through the portal. These appear in your calendar and the Callbacks queue.

### What if I'm double-booked?

Reschedule one of the appointments. Check with both parties for the best alternative time.

### How do I block time for the whole day?

Create a time block from your start time to end time, or mark yourself as Unavailable for that day.

### Can I see who's available for a transfer?

Yes, view the team calendar to see colleagues' availability, then reassign or schedule appropriately.

### What happens to my appointments if I go on leave?

They should be reassigned or rescheduled before your leave starts. Coordinate with your team lead.
