# Getting Started

Welcome to Prowse Phillips CRM! This guide will help you get oriented on your first day and understand the basics of navigating the system.

---

## What is Prowse Phillips CRM?

Prowse Phillips CRM is a debt contention case management system. We help clients who are struggling with debts by investigating whether those debts are legally enforceable. If we find issues with how the debt was set up or managed, we can help clients challenge it or negotiate better terms.

**The system has two main parts:**

1. **Staff CRM (Admin Portal)** - Where you work. This is where you manage clients, cases, documents, and communications.
2. **Client Portal** - Where clients log in to view their case progress, upload documents, and manage payments.

This documentation focuses on the Staff CRM - the system you'll use every day.

## Logging In

### Accessing the System

1. **Open your browser** and go to the admin portal URL (provided by your IT administrator)
2. **Enter your email address** - This is your work email
3. **Enter your password** - Your initial password was provided when your account was created
4. **Click "Sign In"**

[SCREENSHOT: Login page with email and password fields]

### First-Time Login

On your first login, you may be prompted to:
- Change your temporary password
- Review and accept system terms

> **Tip:** Choose a strong password that's unique to this system. You'll use it daily, so make it memorable but secure.

### Password Issues

If you've forgotten your password or your account is locked:
1. Click "Forgot Password" on the login page
2. Enter your email address
3. Check your inbox for a reset link
4. Follow the link to create a new password

If you still can't access your account, contact your IT administrator.

## Staff Roles

Your role determines what you can see and do in the system. There are three main staff roles:

| Role | Capabilities |
|------|--------------|
| **Sales** | View leads and incomplete onboarding, make initial contact with potential clients, limited case visibility |
| **Admin** | Full case management, send correspondence to clients and creditors, manage tasks and documents, view all clients |
| **Solicitor** | Everything Admins can do, plus the authority to approve legal documents like Letters of Claim (LOCs) and settlement proposals |

Your role is assigned when your account is created. If you believe you need different permissions, speak with your manager.

## The Dashboard

After logging in, you land on the dashboard. This is your home base - it shows you what needs your attention today.

[SCREENSHOT: Main dashboard with stats cards and activity feed]

### Dashboard Components

**Stats Cards (Top Row)**

These give you a quick overview of the system:
- **Active Cases** - Total number of cases currently in progress
- **My Cases** - Cases assigned to you
- **Pending Tasks** - Tasks waiting for completion
- **Overdue Items** - Anything past its due date (needs immediate attention)

**My Tasks Section**

Shows your upcoming and overdue tasks. Click any task to go directly to it.

**Recent Activity**

A feed of recent actions across the system - new clients, status changes, documents uploaded. Useful for staying aware of what's happening.

**Quick Actions**

Buttons for common tasks like "Add Client" or "View All Cases".

## Finding Clients and Cases

### Global Search

The search bar at the top of every page lets you quickly find what you need:

1. **Click the search bar** or press `Ctrl+K` (Windows) or `Cmd+K` (Mac)
2. **Type your search term** - client name, email, phone number, or case reference
3. **Review results** - Results are grouped by type (Clients, Cases)
4. **Click a result** to go directly to that record

> **Tip:** Case references follow the format `DC-2024-00123`. You can search by the full reference or just the number portion.

### Using Filters

On list pages (Clients, Cases, Tasks), you'll find filter options:

1. **Click the filter icon** or "Filters" button
2. **Set your criteria** - Status, assigned handler, date range, etc.
3. **Apply filters** - Results update immediately
4. **Clear filters** - Click "Clear All" to reset

Common filter combinations:
- **My assigned cases** - Filter by "Assigned To: Me"
- **Cases needing attention** - Filter by "Status: Awaiting Response"
- **Overdue items** - Filter by "Overdue: Yes"

## Understanding Your Workspace

### List Views

Most sections start with a list view showing all records:

- **Columns** can be sorted by clicking the header
- **Rows** are clickable - click anywhere to open the detail view
- **Actions** appear when you hover over a row (view, edit, etc.)
- **Pagination** at the bottom lets you navigate through large lists

### Detail Views

When you click on a client or case, you see the detail view:

- **Header** shows key information (name, reference, status)
- **Tabs** organize different types of information
- **Action buttons** in the top-right for common operations

### Common Tab Structure

Both client and case detail pages use tabs:

| Tab | What You'll Find |
|-----|------------------|
| **Overview** | Summary information, key stats, recent activity |
| **Cases/Debts** | Related cases (on clients) or debts (on cases) |
| **Documents** | Uploaded and generated documents |
| **Communications** | Email and SMS history |
| **Timeline** | Visual history of all events |
| **Notes** | Internal team notes |
| **Payments** | Payment plan and history |

## Key Terminology

Before diving into workflows, here are essential terms you'll encounter:

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Client** | A person who has signed up for our services |
| **Lead** | A potential client who started onboarding but hasn't completed payment setup |
| **Case** | An investigation into ONE specific debt |
| **Debt** | The money owed to a specific creditor |
| **Creditor** | The company the client owes money to (banks, credit card companies, etc.) |
| **DSAR** | Data Subject Access Request - a formal request for all data a creditor holds about the client |
| **Section 78** | A legal request under the Consumer Credit Act for the original credit agreement |
| **Route U** | "Unenforceable" - when we find the debt cannot be legally enforced |
| **Route E** | "Enforceable" - when the debt is valid but we can negotiate settlements |
| **LOC** | Letter of Claim - a formal legal letter to the creditor |
| **Handler** | The staff member responsible for a case |

## Your First Tasks

Here's what to do on your first day:

### 1. Explore the Dashboard
- Familiarize yourself with the layout
- Note where your assigned tasks appear

- Go to Tasks
- See if you have any assigned tasks
- Understand how tasks link to cases

## Tips for Success

1. **Check your task queue first thing each morning** - This shows what needs your attention today

2. **Use the timeline** - When taking over a case, the timeline shows you everything that's happened

3. **Add notes liberally** - Future you (and your colleagues) will thank you for context

4. **Don't skip statuses** - Cases should progress through each stage in order

5. **Ask questions** - If something seems wrong or unclear, ask before acting

## FAQ

### How do I change my password?

1. Click your name/avatar in the top-right corner
2. Select "Profile" or "Change Password"
3. Enter your current password and new password
4. Click "Save"

### What's the difference between my cases and all cases?

- **My Cases** - Cases where you are the assigned handler
- **All Cases** - Every case in the system (visible based on your role)

Use "My Cases" for your daily work; use "All Cases" when searching for something specific or helping a colleague.

### Where do I see my assigned tasks?

Tasks appear in multiple places:
- **Dashboard** - Your most urgent tasks are shown on the home screen
- **Tasks menu** - Full list of all your tasks with filtering
- **Case detail** - Tasks related to that specific case

### What if I notice an error in client data?

1. **Minor corrections** (typos, formatting) - Edit directly if you have permission
2. **Major changes** (wrong email, significant data) - Note it and inform your team lead
3. **Data discrepancies** - Add a note to the client record explaining the issue

### How do I log out?

Click your name/avatar in the top-right corner and select "Sign Out" or "Logout".
